Hello, my name is Rich.

I‘m 28, am a Christian, live in Sheffield and am very tall. Some people say that I bear an uncanny resemblance to Stephen Merchant (not sure if that‘s a compliment?)

Richard Wells

Stephen Merchant

In my day job I‘m the Creative Director for a digital agency called Yoomee. Working with a lot of charities, we understand the importance of telling stories and the power that social media can have in encouraging people to do good things.

I would absolutely love the opportunity to travel to Cambodia with Tearfund to tell stories and bring people with me on the journey. Here's why...

The power of a picture

We live in a visual culture, and I make lots of images that people engage with. I regularly post original drawings, photos, artwork, and videos to my blog, as well as all the main social networks, and am aware of the power of images to tell stories and keep people with you.

I‘m used to blogging on the move

My blog works on all screens, no matter how big or small. I love how easy it is to create something on my phone or iPad and then share with the world instantly (internet connection allowing!). I imagine this approach would work well on a trip like this.

Give me a challenge!

I started up a blog last year to help young adults get into reading the Bible. It was a 100 day design marathon and ended up being very challenging to keep up, but I loved it, and am itching to do something like it again!

Find out more

I love to explore

I find travelling, meeting different people and exploring other cultures to be hugely exciting, whether that‘s on my doorstep or further afield, and I appreciate the perspective it brings.

Exploring close to home

On Tuesdays I take the day off to explore the world with my daughter Sophie. Our weekly blog has quite a following!

Exploring far away

In 2011 I had an amazing experience meeting Christians in China with a great charity. Unfortunately I‘m not able to publicly share their stories, but I did make a touristy video of some of the sights we saw (all in one second shots so it‘s a bit trippy!)

And so in summary...

Meeting the people of Tonle Batie, understanding what life is like for them and seeing what God is doing there thorough His church would be a huge privilege.

God has been challenging me recently about my consumeristic attitudes and I imagine that meeting people who struggle in poverty would have a big impact on me. In turn, I hope that I would be able to share some of that with the people who follow me online, and in doing so raise up more supporters for the essential work that Tearfund does.
